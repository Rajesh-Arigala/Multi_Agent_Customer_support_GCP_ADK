Intrauterine Insemination (IUI) is a simple, minimally invasive fertility treatment where prepared sperm is directly placed into the uterus around the time of ovulation. It increases the chances of fertilization by bringing sperm closer to the egg. IUI is a procedure where the husband's or donor's (in cases of donor IUI) semen sample is processed in the laboratory to separate motile, healthy sperms and remove dead sperms and debris from the seminal fluid. The processed sample, now rich in motile and healthy sperm, is loaded into a thin catheter and injected directly into the uterus. For couples facing Azoospermia (complete absence of sperm), donor IUI offers a reliable path to parenthood. This process involves using high-quality sperm from reputable banks that have undergone rigorous medical, genetic, and infectious disease screening. Donors undergo extensive medical and genetic testing to ensure safety. Parents can match donor physical characteristics with their own. Strict legal and privacy protections are maintained for all parties. Our specialists provide compassionate support to ensure you feel informed at every step of the donor process.
