IVF success rates are influenced by several key factors. The most important include a woman's age, the quality of eggs and sperm, underlying health conditions, and the specific reasons for infertility. Younger women (typically under age 35) generally have the highest success rates — usually around 40–50% per cycle . For women in their late 30s and 40s, success rates gradually decline due to reduced egg quality and quantity. Previous pregnancy history, lifestyle habits, and overall health also play a significant role in determining outcomes. WHAT CAN I DO TO IMPROVE MY FERTILITY BEFORE TREATMENT? Preparing your body for IVF can significantly improve your chances of success. Experts recommend: Eating a healthy, balanced diet Maintaining an optimal weight Exercising moderately Avoiding smoking and limiting alcohol and caffeine Managing stress and getting adequate sleep Starting prenatal vitamins with folic acid Completing all recommended health screenings Reducing exposure to environmental toxins These steps help optimize egg and sperm quality and support a healthier reproductive environment. AT WHAT AGE SHOULD I START THINKING ABOUT FERTILITY? Fertility naturally begins to decline with age, especially after 35 . Women in their late 20s and early 30s typically have the best fertility potential. If you are planning to conceive later or have medical conditions affecting fertility, it is advisable to consider fertility assessment, ovarian reserve testing, or fertility preservation (such as egg freezing) in your early 30s. Every woman is different, so understanding your reproductive health early can help you plan more confidently for the future. WHAT CAN BE DONE AFTER A FAILED IVF CYCLE? If an IVF cycle fails, additional tests can help identify possible reasons. These may include: Hysteroscopy: Visual examination of the uterine cavity Endometrial Receptivity Assay (ERA): Determines if the uterine lining is receptive Preimplantation Genetic Testing (PGT-A): Screens embryos for chromosomal abnormalities Sperm DNA fragmentation tests Hormonal evaluations Immunological profile assessments These investigations help identify issues related to embryo quality, uterine environment, sperm integrity, or hormonal imbalance, allowing for a more personalized treatment plan for future cycles.
