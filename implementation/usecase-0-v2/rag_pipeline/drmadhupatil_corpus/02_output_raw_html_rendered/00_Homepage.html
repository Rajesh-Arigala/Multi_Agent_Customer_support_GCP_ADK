My Mission and Vision

Dedicated to compassionate women’s health and fertility care, empowering every journey with trust and hope.

Dr. Madhu Patil is a highly experienced gynecologist and IVF specialist with over 13 years in obstetrics and gynecology, including 9+ years dedicated to infertility and assisted reproductive technology (ART). She has successfully guided more than 10,000 couples and performed over 2,000 IVF/ICSI cycles with outcomes above industry standards.

She completed her MBBS at MRMC, Gulbarga, her postgraduate training at GNMC, Aurangabad, and DNB at Deenanath Mangeshkar Hospital, Pune, followed by a Fellowship in ART from KEIL, Germany. Known for her empathetic approach and hands-on care, Dr. Patil also mentors young clinicians and actively promotes women’s health awareness. Fluent in multiple languages, she connects with patients from diverse backgrounds, making her a trusted name in fertility care.

Frequently Asked Questions

IVF success rates are influenced by several key factors. The most important include a woman’s age, the quality of eggs and sperm, underlying health conditions, and the specific reasons for infertility. Younger women (typically under age 35) generally have the highest success rates — usually around 40–50% per cycle. For women in their late 30s and 40s, success rates gradually decline due to reduced egg quality and quantity. Previous pregnancy history, lifestyle habits, and overall health also play a significant role in determining outcomes.

Preparing your body for IVF can significantly improve your chances of success. Experts recommend:

  • Eating a healthy, balanced diet
  • Maintaining an optimal weight
  • Exercising moderately
  • Avoiding smoking and limiting alcohol and caffeine
  • Managing stress and getting adequate sleep
  • Starting prenatal vitamins with folic acid
  • Completing all recommended health screenings
  • Reducing exposure to environmental toxins

These steps help optimize egg and sperm quality and support a healthier reproductive environment.

Fertility naturally begins to decline with age, especially after 35. Women in their late 20s and early 30s typically have the best fertility potential. If you are planning to conceive later or have medical conditions affecting fertility, it is advisable to consider fertility assessment, ovarian reserve testing, or fertility preservation (such as egg freezing) in your early 30s. Every woman is different, so understanding your reproductive health early can help you plan more confidently for the future.

If an IVF cycle fails, additional tests can help identify possible reasons. These may include:

  • Hysteroscopy: Visual examination of the uterine cavity
  • Endometrial Receptivity Assay (ERA): Determines if the uterine lining is receptive
  • Preimplantation Genetic Testing (PGT-A): Screens embryos for chromosomal abnormalities
  • Sperm DNA fragmentation tests
  • Hormonal evaluations
  • Immunological profile assessments

These investigations help identify issues related to embryo quality, uterine environment, sperm integrity, or hormonal imbalance, allowing for a more personalized treatment plan for future cycles.

Men with low sperm count have multiple treatment options to achieve pregnancy. After a thorough evaluation, treatment may include lifestyle modifications, medical therapy to address hormonal or underlying conditions, or advanced assisted reproductive techniques. Procedures such as intrauterine insemination (IUI) may be effective in mild cases, while in vitro fertilization (IVF) with intracytoplasmic sperm injection (ICSI) offers high success rates even with very low sperm counts. With individualized care and advanced technology, many couples can successfully conceive despite male factor infertility.

Men with zero sperm count (azoospermia) have treatment options based on the underlying cause. After detailed evaluation, azoospermia may be obstructive or non-obstructive. Treatment may include medical or hormonal therapy, surgical correction of blockages, or advanced sperm retrieval procedures such as TESA, TESE, or micro-TESE. When sperm are successfully retrieved, pregnancy can be achieved using IVF with intracytoplasmic sperm injection (ICSI). With specialized fertility care and advanced technology, many men with azoospermia are able to achieve biological parenthood.
