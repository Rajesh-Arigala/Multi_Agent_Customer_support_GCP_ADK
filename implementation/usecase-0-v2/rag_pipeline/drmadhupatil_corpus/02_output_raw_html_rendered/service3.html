What is IUI?

Intrauterine Insemination (IUI) is a simple, minimally invasive fertility treatment where prepared sperm is directly placed into the uterus around the time of ovulation. It increases the chances of fertilization by bringing sperm closer to the egg.

IUI is a procedure where the husband's or donor's (in cases of donor IUI) semen sample is processed in the laboratory to separate motile, healthy sperms and remove dead sperms and debris from the seminal fluid. The processed sample, now rich in motile and healthy sperm, is loaded into a thin catheter and injected directly into the uterus.

Donor IUI for Azoospermia

For couples facing Azoospermia (complete absence of sperm), donor IUI offers a reliable path to parenthood. This process involves using high-quality sperm from reputable banks that have undergone rigorous medical, genetic, and infectious disease screening.

IUI Treatment Steps

Step 1: Initial Evaluation & Planning

  • Medical evaluation of both partners
  • Blood tests and ultrasound scans
  • Personalized treatment plan creation

Step 2: Ovulation Monitoring & Triggering

  • Natural cycle IUI requires no stimulation
  • Stimulated cycles use fertility medications
  • Ultrasound monitoring of follicle growth
  • Trigger injection when follicles reach optimal size

Step 3: Sperm Preparation

  • Semen collection from partner or donor
  • Laboratory processing to select healthiest sperm
  • Removal of seminal fluid and debris
  • Concentration of motile sperm

Step 4: Insemination Procedure

  • Quick and usually painless procedure
  • Patient lies on exam table
  • Speculum inserted for cervical visualization
  • Thin catheter passes washed sperm into uterus

Step 5: Aftercare & Luteal Phase Support

  • Brief rest in clinic after procedure
  • Resume normal activities same day
  • Pregnancy test two weeks later

Natural Process

Lower Risk

Fewer complications than more advanced treatments

Risks of IUI

Multiple Pregnancy

Risk of twins if ovulation induction is used

OHSS

Ovarian Hyperstimulation Syndrome (rare with modern protocols)

Mild Discomfort

Cramping or spotting after insemination

Infection Risk

Very low risk of infection from the procedure

Note: OHSS is rare these days due to advances in protocols and careful monitoring during treatment. For donor IUI, additional considerations include emotional aspects and legal agreements.
